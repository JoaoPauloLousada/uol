Visualising research with concept diagrams
This activity challenges you to apply the innovative visualisation techniques proposed in Sean's recent research paper on concept diagrams. By translating research findings into a compelling visual format, you will gain practical experience in communicating complex information effectively and creatively.

Task 1: Explore the research

Familiarise yourself with Sean's research paper on concept diagrams, available here: 

McGrath, S. et al.  Evaluating colour in concept diagrams. In: Giardino, V., S. Linker, R. Burns, F. Bellucci, J.M. Boucheix and P. Viana (eds) Diagrammatic representation and inference. Diagrams (2022). Lecture Notes in Computer Science 13462 (Cham: Springer, 2022).

Task 2: Select a focus

Choose one ‘main concept’ or key finding from the research paper that you want to visualise. 

Alternatively, select a few visualisation techniques from the paper to showcase their application.

Try to think about a concept you could visualise using these techniques. They lend themselves well to complex relationships - the types of things you work on in data mining, set theory and other complex computing domains. But you could pick a novel concept like visualising the hierarchy of the University, a musical structure or even how your pets interact with one another!

Step 3: Design a poster

Create an A4 poster that effectively communicates your chosen concept or techniques applied to a domain of your choosing.

Sketch out your poster design and plan your layout before creating the final version

Employ visual elements like diagrams, charts and illustrations inspired by the research paper. The choice of colours is up to you. 

Choose a colour scheme that enhances readability and visual appeal. One option is to use the accessible colour palette that Sean chose but you are not constrained by this. 

Ensure your poster is clear, concise (do not use excessive amounts of text) and visually engaging. 

Task 4: Share your poster

Once you’ve created your poster, share it on the discussion forum. To do this, you will have to export your poster as an image.. Give feedback to at least one of your peers’ posters, using the following criteria to structure your feedback: 

Clarity of communication: How effectively does the poster convey the chosen concept or techniques? 

Visual appeal: Is the poster visually engaging and well-designed? 

Originality: Does the poster demonstrate creative application of the visualisation techniques? 

Accuracy: Is the information presented accurate and consistent with the research paper?
